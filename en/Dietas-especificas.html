Mediterranean Diet

Characteristics: Inspired by the eating patterns of Mediterranean countries such as Greece and Italy, this diet is based on abundant consumption of fruits, vegetables, legumes, nuts, whole grains, fish, and olive oil. Red meat, dairy products, and processed foods are limited. Moderate consumption of red wine is also included.

Benefits: It has been associated with a reduced risk of heart diseases, type 2 diabetes, and certain types of cancer. It is also believed to promote longevity and brain health.

Ketogenic Diet:

Characteristics: This diet is high in fats, moderate in proteins, and very low in carbohydrates. The goal is to induce a state of ketosis, where the body burns fats instead of carbohydrates as the main source of energy. Foods such as meat, fish, eggs, avocados, healthy oils, and certain low-carb vegetables are included, while grains, fruits, and sugars are limited.

Benefits: It has been shown to aid in weight loss, improve blood sugar levels in individuals with type 2 diabetes, and may have positive effects on neurological disorders such as epilepsy.

Vegetarian Diet:

Characteristics: Vegetarians exclude meat and fish from their diet but may include dairy products and eggs. The diet is based on fruits, vegetables, legumes, whole grains, nuts, and seeds.

Benefits: It has been associated with a lower risk of heart diseases, hypertension, type 2 diabetes, and certain types of cancer. Additionally, it may be more environmentally sustainable than diets that include meat.

Vegan Diet:

Characteristics: Similar to the vegetarian diet, but vegans exclude all animal products including dairy, eggs, and honey. It is based on fruits, vegetables, legumes, whole grains, nuts, and seeds.

Benefits: It has been associated with a reduced risk of chronic diseases such as heart diseases, type 2 diabetes, and certain types of cancer. It can also be more sustainable and ethical than diets that include animal products.
