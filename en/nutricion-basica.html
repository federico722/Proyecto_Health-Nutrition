Basic Nutrition

Basic nutrition refers to the essential principles that guide a balanced and healthy diet to maintain optimal health. Below are the fundamental concepts:

Food Groups: A balanced diet includes a variety of foods from different groups to ensure adequate intake of essential nutrients. These groups typically include:

Grains and cereals: Main source of complex carbohydrates, fiber, and some B vitamins.

Fruits and vegetables: Abundant in vitamins, minerals, fiber, and antioxidants that are essential for health.

Proteins: From sources such as lean meats, fish, eggs, legumes, and dairy products, they are necessary for tissue repair and growth.

Dairy or alternatives: Important sources of calcium and proteins, essential for bone health and muscle development.

Healthy fats: Include unsaturated fats found in vegetable oils, nuts, seeds, and fish, which are necessary for cardiovascular health and brain function.

Balance and Moderation:

The key to a healthy diet lies in balance and moderation. It's important to consume adequate portions of each food group to avoid nutritional deficiencies and caloric excess.

Variety and Color:

A varied and colorful diet ensures the intake of a wide range of nutrients. It is recommended to consume fruits and vegetables of different colors, as each color indicates the presence of different vitamins and antioxidants.

Hydration:

Water is essential to maintain proper hydration and optimal body function. It is recommended to consume at least 8 glasses of water a day, although needs may vary depending on age, weight, and level of physical activity.

Avoid Processed and Sugary Foods:

Limiting the consumption of processed foods, high in trans fats, added sugars, and sodium, can increase the risk of chronic diseases such as obesity, diabetes, and cardiovascular diseases.

Meal Planning:

Planning meals in advance can help maintain a balanced diet and avoid unhealthy impulsive decisions. It is recommended to include a variety of foods in each meal to ensure complete nutrition.

Nutritional Education

Acquiring knowledge about nutrition is essential to make informed decisions about food. This includes reading food labels, understanding nutritional recommendations, and seeking professional advice when necessary.
